1. The Power of Habit

Our lives are largely defined by our habits. From the moment we wake up until we go to sleep, we follow routines that often go unnoticed. But these automatic behaviors hold immense power over our future.

The Habit Loop

According to Charles Duhigg, habits are formed through a three-step loop: the cue, the routine, and the reward. The cue triggers your brain to enter an automatic mode, the routine is the behavior itself, and the reward helps your brain figure out if this particular loop is worth remembering for the future.

How to Change Habits

To change a bad habit, you shouldn't try to extinguish it entirely. Instead, you should keep the cue and the reward, but insert a new routine. For example, if you eat junk food when you are stressed (cue: stress, reward: relaxation), try replacing the junk food with a 5-minute walk. You still get the relaxation, but the routine is healthier.

The Compound Effect

Small changes in your daily routine may seem insignificant today, but they compound over time. Improving just 1% every day can lead to massive progress in a year. Success is not a one-time event; it's the result of your daily habits.
