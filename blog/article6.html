6. Social Influence: Why We Conform

Our social interactions can have a significant impact on our behavior and beliefs. Understanding social influence can help us make more informed decisions and resist peer pressure.

Group Norms and Conformity

Conformity is the tendency to follow group norms and behavior to fit in. We may conform to avoid social rejection or gain acceptance from others.

Peer Pressure

Peer pressure is a powerful form of social influence that can be both positive and negative. It can encourage us to adopt positive behaviors or engage in harmful actions.

The Power of Autonomy

Developing a sense of autonomy and self-reliance can help us resist negative social influence and make more authentic decisions.
